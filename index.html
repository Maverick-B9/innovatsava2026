§ 07 — Competitions

Detailed Event Sections

Full breakdown of all five competitions and showcases at Innovotsava 2026.

🤖
Robotics & Autonomous Systems

The Rise of Autobots

🏟️ Day 1 👥 20 Teams ⏱️ 12 min demo + 3 min Q&A 👤 4 Members/Team
Competition Overview

Design, build, and demonstrate autonomous or semi-autonomous robotic systems that address a real-world problem. Focus on innovation in hardware design, software intelligence, and practical application. Teams present a live working demonstration to a judge panel.

Register for Robotics Competition
Focus Areas
Industrial Automation Agricultural Robotics Healthcare & Assistive Tech Search & Rescue Open Innovation
Technical Requirements
  • Any programming language or microcontroller permitted (Arduino, RPi, ROS, etc.)
  • Battery-powered only — no mains connection during demo
  • Max dimensions: 60×60×80 cm (stationary). Flying robots not allowed in this category
  • Max weight: 5 kg (heavier builds need prior written approval)
  • Remote-controlled allowed; full autonomy scores higher
  • Bring own tools, spare parts, and charging equipment
Judging Scorecard
Criterion Max
Innovation & Originality 25
Technical Complexity 20
Functionality & Live Demo 25
Real-World Relevance 15
Presentation & Communication 15
TOTAL 100
🚫
Key Restrictions:
No pre-built commercial robots. No paid commercial software. Any robot causing arena damage = immediate disqualification. No re-runs once slot is over.
✅ Open-source libraries and frameworks are fully permitted.

Unmanned Aerial Vehicles & Aerial Robotics

Drone Fare

🏟️ Day 1 👥 20 Teams ⏱️ 12 min demo + 3 min Q&A 👤 4 Members/Team
Competition Overview

Design, build, and demonstrate UAV-based systems that solve an identified problem or complete a defined aerial mission. Evaluated on engineering design, flight stability, payload management, autonomous capabilities, and safety compliance.

Register for Drone Fare
Focus Areas
Precision Agriculture Search & Rescue Infrastructure Inspection Surveillance & Mapping Payload Delivery Open Innovation
Safety & Compliance (Mandatory)
  • All drones must comply with DGCA guidelines for educational/hobbyist UAVs
  • Drones above 250g must carry valid DGCA registration — present at check-in
  • Safety officer inspects each drone before flight clearance
  • Flight arena cordoned with safety nets — no flying outside designated zone
  • Physical kill-switch must be functional and tested before slot
  • LiPo batteries in safe bags during transport; charge only in designated zones
  • Max altitude within arena: 30 metres
  • No FPV goggles during public demonstrations
Judging Scorecard
Criterion Max
Mission Achievement & Flight Performance 25
Technical Design & Build Quality 20
Autonomy & Intelligence 20
Safety Compliance 15
Innovation & Application Relevance 10
Presentation & Q&A 10
TOTAL 100
⚠️ Any drone crash posing risk to persons or property results in immediate disqualification. Do not modify drone hardware between inspection and demo slot.
✅ Do's
  • Full pre-flight check before demo
  • Carry spare batteries & propellers
  • Inform safety officer of issues
❌ Don'ts
  • No FPV goggles in demo
  • No flight if kill-switch fails
  • No hardware mods after check-in

AI Agents, Game Development & Intelligent Systems

Game Mania

🏟️ Day 1 👥 20 Teams ⏱️ 12 min demo + 3 min Q&A 👤 4 Members/Team
Competition Overview

Build intelligent game environments, AI-driven game agents, or interactive simulation platforms. Focus is on how AI is integrated into gameplay — as an opponent, simulation engine, or adaptive system. This is a development and demonstration competition, not a competitive gaming event.

Cross-department members from Design, Mechanical, Mathematics, or Electrical backgrounds are especially encouraged.

Register for Game Mania
Focus Areas
AI Game Agents & NPCs Simulation Environments Educational Games with AI AI for Accessibility Generative AI in Games Open Innovation
Technical Requirements
  • Game must be entirely built by the team. Asset packs and open-source engines (Unity, Godot, Unreal, Pygame) permitted
  • AI components must be custom-coded — plug-in LLMs without modification not permitted
  • Present on your own laptop — no cloud streaming
  • Minimum resolution: 720p. Standalone mode (no active internet) required
Judging Scorecard
Criterion Max
AI Depth & Intelligence 30
Gameplay Innovation 20
Technical Execution 20
User Experience 15
Presentation & Q&A 15
TOTAL 100

The flagship entrepreneurship platform of Innovotsava 2026. Teams conceptualize, design, and pitch AI-powered business ventures addressing real-world problems across five defined domains. Top 10 teams advance to Day 2 Finals judged by industry leaders.

Pitch Structure (12 min)

  • Solution Overview — The AI-powered solution and how it works (2–3 min)
  • Market & Impact — Target market, users, social/economic impact (2 min)
  • Business Model — Revenue streams, go-to-market strategy (2 min)
  • Technology Stack — AI/ML methods, data sources, architecture (2 min)
  • Traction & Roadmap — Current progress, prototype, future milestones (1 min)

Judging Framework & Ethics

Event Judges/Panel Judge Profile
Rise of Autobots 3 per batch Robotics faculty, industry engineers

Business Conclave Prelims 3 per batch Entrepreneurs, business faculty

⚖️
Process & Tiebreakers
  • All judges briefed on scoring rubric before events begin
  • Scores recorded on standardized scoresheets by Technical Team

  • Reserve judge available for each panel
  • Results are final once announced — no individual score appeals
  • Procedural grievances may be submitted in writing within 30 minutes of announcement
  • Core Committee reviews procedural grievances within 24 hours

§ 14 — Conduct

Code of Conduct & Policies

📌 All participants, volunteers, company representatives, and guests are expected to adhere to this code. Violations result in disqualification and removal from the event.
✅
Expected Conduct
  • Treat all individuals with respect regardless of gender, department, or institution
  • Engage in fair, honest competition — present only original work
  • Follow all instructions from organizing staff, marshals, and safety officers

§ 15 — Safety

Emergency Protocols & Safety

🚨 In any emergency — medical, fire, or security — contact the nearest marshal immediately. First-aid stations are located at the main venue entrance and near the Drone Fare arena.

🏥 Medical Emergencies

  • First-aid station at main venue entrance & near Drone Fare arena
  • Trained first-aid responders on-site full duration of both days
  • Contact nearest marshal immediately for serious emergencies
  • Do not move the individual unless directed by medical personnel
  • Nearest hospital: To be confirmed by organizers

🔥 Fire & Evacuation

  • Fire exits clearly marked — must not be obstructed at any time
  • On fire alarm: evacuate immediately via nearest marked exit
  • Assembly point: Designated open ground (marked on Day 1 briefing map)
  • Do not attempt to retrieve equipment during evacuation

⚡ Electrical Safety

  • Only authorized personnel handle stage electrical equipment, PA systems, and lighting
  • Participants may not use venue power outlets without Logistics Team approval
  • Drone battery charging only at designated LiPo-safe charging stations

👥 Crowd Management

  • Max capacity of Open Air Theatre: 2,500 — strictly enforced
  • Volunteer marshals stationed at all entry/exit points
  • Barricaded audience zones in effect during Drone Fare flight demos
§ 16 — FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common participant queries about Innovotsava 2026.

Can I register in more than one competition? +
No. A student may not be part of more than one competition team across all events at Innovotsava 2026. Each student can only register in a single competition category in one day.
What is the cross-departmental requirement? +
Every team of 4 must include at least 1 member from a department other than their primary department. This rule applies to all competitions — Robotics, Drone Fare, Game Mania, Business Conclave, and Student Project Expo.
Who is eligible to participate? +
All team members must be currently enrolled students of Maharaja Institute of Technology Mysore (MITM). The event is internal to MITM students only; external participants from other colleges are not eligible for the competitions.
When are the Day 1 competition results announced? +
Results for Rise of Autobots, Drone Fare, and Game Mania are announced during the Networking Session at 5:00 PM on Day 1. Top 10 Business Conclave teams advancing to Finals are also announced at this time. Formal prize distribution happens at the Day 2 Valedictory Ceremony.
Is accommodation provided for outstation participants? +
Outstation participants are responsible for their own accommodation. MITM Guest House bookings may be facilitated on prior request to the organizing committee, subject to availability. Transportation from the Manipal bus stand to MITM campus will be arranged on both event days.
Is food provided at the event? +
No.
What documents do I need to bring on Day 1? +
Bring your valid college ID, your registration confirmation email (digital or printed), and the Faculty Advisor Sign-Off Form. Drone participants must additionally carry DGCA-compliant documentation for their UAV. Report at least 45 minutes before your scheduled slot.
Will certificates be provided? +
Certificates are distributed at the Valedictory Ceremony to all finalists and special mention awardees across all competitions. Business Conclave finalists also receive a Finalists' Certificate and will be listed in the Innovotsava 2026 official publication.
Can I switch my faculty advisor? +
Teams may not switch advisors once assigned without written approval from the Core Committee. The advisor is assigned by the Technical Team during the registration confirmation phase.
Do the Business Conclave Finals teams need to prepare new slides? +
Teams should incorporate feedback received informally after their Day 1 presentation where possible. The audience on Day 2 includes the full crowd of 3,000, so presentations must be clear to a non-technical audience as well. A 2-minute setup window is provided before each team's slot.
